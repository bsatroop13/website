For Scouts & Families

Medical Forms

Required

Every scout needs a current BSA Annual Health & Medical Record (Parts A, B1, B2, and C). Scouts without a properly completed form will be sent home after 24 hours.

  • Parent/guardian AND youth signatures on Part A.
  • Medical evaluation by a physician within 12 months of camp.
  • Insurance info on Part B or insurance card copy (front & back) attached.
  • Tetanus shot within 10 years of July 2026.
  • Restrictions clearly marked.

Medications

  • All meds in original bottles.
  • Each scout's meds in a clear plastic bag labeled: name, when to take, how much.
  • Collected at Sunday check-in, distributed by Health Officer all week.
  • Scouts with epi-pens or inhalers carry them in their day bag and must know how to use them.

Pre-Requisites

Do Before Camp

Each scout's merit badge pre-requisites are specific to their schedule this year. If the pre-work isn't done, the badge can't be finished during the week — bring any written work, projects, or materials with you.

📲

Use the Camp Prep app to check off your personal pre-reqs as you go — tap your name to see your list.

ATV Riders: Kyle & Adam

  • Completed participation & hold-harmless waiver AND ASI General Release (both signed by scout AND parent).
  • Online pre-course at cbt.svia.org — print the completion certificate. Takes 1–2 hours.
  • Long sleeve shirt, long pants, over-the-ankle shoes/boots.
  • $35 fee at check-in.

Finishing a Partial Badge

  • If a scout is finishing a badge they started but didn't complete previously (a "partial"), bring the blue card to camp.
  • Present it to the Program Area Director for that badge on Sunday or Monday — they'll work out with the scout what's left to do and when.
  • Previously completed work may or may not be accepted as-is — the counselor can ask the scout to demonstrate the knowledge or skill again, even with a signed partial card.
  • Partials are valid until the scout turns 18, as long as they can show they're still current on the material.

At Camp

Our Site: Lenape

Shared w/ Troop 265

Lenape (capacity 16) has running water, canvas tents on raised platforms with cots, picnic tables, fire cans, a flag pole, and latrine. Since we're sharing, our SPL will meet with Troop 265's SPL on Sunday to set ground rules.

Arrive together between 1:00–3:00 PM. Do not arrive before 1 PM. Park in the Shale Parking Lot. Once scouts connect with troop leadership, drivers are encouraged to head home. Camp is not open to visitors during the week except in an emergency.

  • Have everyone in Class A or troop shirts for the optional troop photo on arrival.
  • Scouts → campsite → swim gear → medical rechecks → swim checks → dining hall & range orientation.
  • 5:50 PM — Evening Colors (Class A). 8:30 PM — Welcome Campfire.

Bears & Food Policy

Strict

No food, snacks, beverages, wrappers, or anything that smells like food in or near campsites. Bears wander through camp regularly. All trash to the dumpsters behind the Dining Hall. Snacks stored in a locked vehicle; brought to the site in small amounts and consumed immediately.

Good to Know

  • Trading post: Camp gear, snacks, drinks, slushies, ice cream. Accepts cash, cards, and tap-to-pay. Bring spending money in a labeled wallet and lock it up.
  • Showers: Central shower house is close to our site at Lenape. Bring closed-toe shower shoes.
  • Polar Bear Swim Club: Mon/Wed/Fri at 6:30 AM. Attend all 3 for the patch.
  • COPE: Patrol Try-Out at 2 PM daily. Full COPE Adventure 1–3 PM all week, ages 13+.
  • Troop photo: Taken at the Main Gate on Sunday arrival. Formal 8×10 ($7) and silly 5×7 ($4). Let your leader know if you want to order.

Saturday Return

Leaders will drive all scouts back to Scout Hall in Trumbauersville on Saturday, July 25. If you need to make other arrangements for pickup or drop-off, text a leader in advance.
