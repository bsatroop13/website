For Scouts & Families

Medical Forms

Required

Every scout needs a current BSA Annual Health & Medical Record (Parts A, B1, B2, and C). Scouts without a properly completed form will not be allowed to attend.

We need three copies of each form: one for camp staff, one for Troop 13 leaders, and one to keep on file for the 2026–27 scout year.

  • Parent/guardian AND youth signatures on Part A.
  • Insurance card copy (front & back) attached.
  • Immunization records included.
  • Restrictions clearly marked.

Medications

  • All medications in original bottles, bagged and labeled with the scout's name, dosage, and schedule.
  • Collected at Sunday check-in and distributed by camp medical staff throughout the week.
  • Epi-pens and inhalers only: scouts carry these in their day bag and must know how to use them.

Pre-Requisites

Do Before Camp

Each scout's merit badge pre-requisites are specific to their schedule this year. If the pre-work isn't done, the badge can't be finished during the week — bring any written work, projects, or materials with you.

ATV Riders: Kyle & Adam

The ATV packet contains two forms that must be completed and signed by both the scout and a parent/guardian before Sunday:

  • ASI ATV Safety Course General Release & Indemnification Agreement
  • Scouting America Participation & Hold-Harmless Agreement
  • Complete the online pre-course at cbt.svia.org and print the completion certificate. Takes 1–2 hours. No registration or payment required — do not pay for the "State Certificate."
  • Long sleeve shirt, long pants, over-the-ankle shoes or boots.
  • $35 fee due at check-in. Make checks payable to Northeastern PA Council, BSA.
  • Note: if a scout is signed up but hasn't completed the pre-reqs, the fee is still owed if the slot can't be filled.

Finishing a Partial Badge

  • If a scout is finishing a badge they started but didn't complete previously, bring the blue card to camp.
  • Present it to the Program Area Director for that badge on Sunday or Monday — they'll work out with the scout what's left to do and when.
  • Previously completed work may or may not be accepted as-is. The counselor can ask the scout to demonstrate knowledge or skills again, even with a signed partial card.
  • Partials are valid until the scout turns 18, as long as they can show they're still current on the material.

Do NOT Bring

Prohibited

Fireworks. Firearms and ammo. Sheath knives. Alcohol. Drugs. Tobacco and vape. Aerosol cans. Glass containers. Candles and open flames. Pets. Gasoline and kerosene. Personal bikes (unless Cycling MB). No food in campsites — bears are active in the area.

At Camp

Our Site: Lenape

Shared w/ Troop 265

Lenape is a smaller site centrally located in camp, just off Applegate Lane across from the Helcoski/Handicraft Lodge — within easy walking distance of just about everything. Since we're sharing, our SPL will meet with Troop 265's SPL on Sunday to set ground rules.

  • 8 two-man canvas tent platforms, each with 2 metal-framed cots and mattresses
  • Program tarp and dining fly
  • 1 picnic table
  • Wooded center area with good shade coverage
  • On-site latrine and washstand with flush toilets

Bears & Food Policy

Strict

No food, snacks, beverages, wrappers, or anything that smells like food in or near campsites. Bears wander through camp regularly. All trash goes to the dumpsters behind the Dining Hall. If you have extra food or snacks, give them to a leader to store securely.

Good to Know

  • Trading post: camp gear, snacks, drinks, slushies, ice cream. Accepts cash, cards, and tap-to-pay.
  • Showers: the shower house is close to our site. Bring closed-toe shower shoes.
  • Polar Bear Swim Club: Mon/Wed/Fri at 6:30 AM. Attend all 3 for the patch.
  • COPE: Patrol Try-Out at 2 PM daily. Full COPE Adventure 1–3 PM all week, ages 13+.
  • Troop photo: taken at the Main Gate on Sunday arrival. Formal 8×10 ($7) and silly 5×7 ($4). Let your leader know if you want to order.

Saturday Return

Leaders will drive all scouts back to Scout Hall in Trumbauersville on Saturday, July 25. If you need to make other arrangements for pickup or drop-off, text a leader in advance.
